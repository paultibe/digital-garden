## Being effective is more important than being efficient.

The belief guiding my philosophy of intentionality.

“Effectiveness is doing the things that get you closer to your goals. Efficiency is performing a given task (whether important or not) in the most economical manner possible.” - Tim Ferris

Moving in the right direction is more important than the speed with which you’re moving. Don’t just blindly go from A to B (that’s right in front of you). Think carefully about what the right B is.

The “what” that is right will depend on your values, goals, priorities. This is a point about continuously going inwards to identify the right things to invest time in.

Don’t only sit in the comfort of convergent thinking; grow comfortable with the discomfort of divergent thinking. People often stay busy because they’re too afraid to confront themselves and face that they may be doing the wrong thing.

Learn to separate yourself into two agents. The planner and the doer. The boss and the employee. The planner thinks critically about what’s important across longer time frames like a week, a month, a year, and the doer follows the planner.
* The doer is action biased and doesn’t obsess over perfect action.
- The planner asks: “If this is the single most important thing I accomplish today, will I be satisfied”

"Being selective is the path of the productive. Focus on the important few and ignore the rest." - Tim Ferris
